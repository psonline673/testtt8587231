FAQS

Can I use a regular household vacuum cleaner to clean heavy debris from the bottom of my pool?

No, regular household vacuum cleaners are not designed for pool cleaning and could get damaged if used in water. It is recommended to use a pool vacuum specifically designed for cleaning pools.

How often should I vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of my pool?

It is recommended to vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of your pool at least once a week to prevent buildup and maintain optimal water quality.

What is the best time to vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of my pool?

The best time to vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of your pool is in the morning or early evening when the sun is not directly overhead to avoid excessive evaporation of water during the cleaning process.

Can I vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of my pool manually?

Yes, you can manually vacuum heavy debris from the bottom of your pool using a pool vacuum with a telescopic pole for reaching the bottom easily. However, for larger pools or heavy debris, an automatic pool vacuum may be more efficient.

How do I prevent heavy debris from sinking to the bottom of my pool in the first place?

To prevent heavy debris from sinking to the bottom of your pool, regularly skim the surface of the water with a pool skimmer to remove leaves and other debris before they sink. Additionally, maintaining proper chemical balance in your pool can help prevent debris accumulation.
