Biloxi Lighthouse

Biloxi Lighthouse stands tall as a historic beacon along the Gulf Coast, captivating visitors with its striking white structure against the backdrop of the azure waters. Originally built in 1848, this iconic landmark has weathered countless storms and witnessed the evolution of Biloxi over the years. As the only cast-iron lighthouse in the United States, it offers a glimpse into the maritime history of the region, serving as a symbol of resilience and strength.

Visitors can ascend the spiral staircase to the top of the Biloxi Lighthouse and witness panoramic views of the coastline and bustling city below. The breathtaking vistas from the observation deck make the climb worth every step, providing a mesmerizing perspective of the surrounding landscape. Whether admiring the sunset over the Mississippi Sound or gazing at the endless expanse of the Gulf of Mexico, the Biloxi Lighthouse offers a serene retreat for those seeking a moment of tranquility amidst the vibrant energy of the Gulf Coast.

Maritime and Seafood Industry Museum

The Maritime and Seafood Industry Museum in Biloxi, Mississippi, immerses visitors in the rich maritime history of the region. Through interactive exhibits and captivating displays, the museum showcases the integral role that the seafood industry has played in shaping the culture and economy of the Gulf Coast. From learning about the techniques used in shrimping and oystering to exploring the evolution of boat-building in the area, visitors can gain a deeper appreciation for the traditions and innovations that have defined the maritime heritage of Biloxi.

Visitors to the museum can also explore the vibrant history of Biloxi's seafood industry, from its early beginnings to its present-day impact. With artifacts, photographs, and stories highlighting the resilience and resourcefulness of those who have made their living from the sea, the museum offers a poignant tribute to the generations of fishermen and seafood workers who have helped to make Biloxi a hub of maritime activity. Whether you're a history enthusiast or simply curious about the intricate connections between seafood and culture, the Maritime and Seafood Industry Museum is a must-visit destination in Biloxi.

Mullet Toss at FloraBama

The annual Mullet Toss at Flora-Bama is a unique and lively event that draws both locals and tourists alike to the shores of the Gulf Coast. Participants of all ages gather to test their strength and skills by tossing a mullet fish across the state line from Alabama into Florida, amidst cheers and laughter. The event not only showcases the local tradition of mullet fishing but also fosters a sense of community and camaraderie among attendees.

Aside from the actual tossing of the mullet, the Mullet Toss at Flora-Bama offers a festive atmosphere with live music, delicious seafood vendors, and a wide array of activities for everyone to enjoy. Whether you're a competitive mullet tosser or simply a spectator taking in the fun-filled spectacle, this event is a must-visit when in the Biloxi area. Join in the excitement of the Mullet Toss and experience a slice of Gulf Coast culture like never before.
