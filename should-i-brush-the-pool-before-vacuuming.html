FAQS

Should I brush the pool before vacuuming?

Yes, it is recommended to brush the pool before vacuuming. Brushing helps loosen up dirt and algae that may be stuck to the walls and floor of the pool, making it easier for the vacuum to remove them.

How often should I brush my pool before vacuuming?

It is recommended to brush your pool at least once a week before vacuuming. However, if you notice a buildup of debris or algae, you may need to brush more frequently.

Can I skip brushing and just rely on vacuuming?

While vacuuming is important for removing debris, brushing is equally important as it helps prevent algae growth and keeps the pool surfaces clean. Skipping brushing can lead to dirt and algae buildup, making it harder to clean the pool in the future.

What type of brush should I use to brush my pool?

The type of brush you use will depend on the surface of your pool. For concrete or gunite pools, use a stiff brush, while for vinyl or fiberglass pools, use a softer brush to avoid scratching the surface.

Can I use an automatic pool cleaner instead of manually brushing and vacuuming?

Automatic pool cleaners can be a great addition to your pool maintenance routine, but they may not be able to reach all areas of the pool or provide the same level of thorough cleaning as manual brushing and vacuuming. It is still recommended to manually brush the pool before using an automatic cleaner for best results.
